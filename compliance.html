Compliance

How we think about regulatory alignment and financial controls.

Wintellex operates inside a regulated financial ecosystem. This page explains our approach to oversight, data protection, and the banking relationships that underpin our infrastructure — not as a checklist, but as how we build.

Last reviewed June 2026
Our Approach

Compliance as infrastructure, not paperwork.

We treat regulatory alignment as a design constraint that shapes how Wintellex is built, not a layer added after the fact.

01

Built around oversight

Our systems are designed so that activity is reviewable by design — every workflow produces a record, and every record has an owner.

02

Bank-partnered by design

Wintellex is a technology layer, not a bank. Funds-related services run through chartered, regulated banking partners, with clear lines of responsibility.

03

Reviewed on an ongoing basis

Controls, access policies, and vendor relationships are revisited on a recurring cycle rather than treated as a one-time setup.

Where This Shows Up

The areas compliance touches across our platform.

Access governance

Permissions are role-based and scoped to what a person or system actually needs. Sensitive actions — moving funds, changing settlement rules, exporting records — require defined approval steps rather than standing access.

Audit visibility

Key actions across payments, treasury, and settlement workflows are logged in a way that supports internal review and, where applicable, examination by our banking partners or their regulators.

Data handling

We collect what's needed to operate the platform and retain it according to documented schedules. Access to sensitive financial data is restricted and monitored, in line with our Privacy Policy.

Developer & API governance

API access follows the same principle as internal access: scoped keys, environment separation between sandbox and production, and the ability to revoke access immediately when needed.

Vendor & partner review

Banking, infrastructure, and processing partners are evaluated before integration and reassessed periodically, rather than treated as a fixed dependency set.

Banking Relationship

Wintellex is technology. Funds movement runs through chartered banks.

Wintellex does not hold a banking charter and is not itself a bank. Banking services — including deposit accounts and underlying funds movement where applicable — are provided by our chartered banking partners, each a member of the FDIC.

Data & Privacy

Financial data deserves a higher bar.

Payment and treasury data is sensitive by nature. Our approach to handling it is detailed in full in our Privacy Policy — the points below are a summary of how that translates into practice.

＋

Minimal collection

We collect what's necessary to operate payments, treasury, and compliance workflows — not more.

＋

Restricted access

Internal access to financial records is limited to roles that need it, and that access is logged.

＋

Defined retention

Data is kept according to documented retention schedules, not indefinitely by default.

＋

Third-party transparency

Where data is shared with partners — banking, processing, or infrastructure — that relationship is disclosed in our privacy practices.
